CO2 Emissions Data Visualization

Final Project for ARTG 5300

Global carbon emissions have significantly increased since the 1900s. The visualization below
shows the relationship between the yearly CO2 emissions per capita and the population. The
bubbles depict 10 countries with population 5 million and above. Radius of the bubbles shows
the GDP per capita of each country. The tooltip function provides information about the total
annual emissions of each country and the GDP per capita. The aim of the visualization is to
depict the correlation between population and the amount of CO2 emissions.

Data about population was gathered from the World Bank, while the data about the
emissions is available on Our World in Data.

The circles size of each circle depends on each country's GDP per capita value. Another important layer of information is the number of total annual CO2 emissions of each country.
