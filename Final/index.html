The visualization below shows the relationship between the yearly CO2 emissions per capita and the population.
The bubbles depict 10 countries with population 5 million and above.

Global carbon emissions have significantly increased since the 1900s. The wealthiest countries
of the world are home to half of the world population, and emit 86 percent of CO2 emissions.
Factors that determine collective CO2 emissions are the number of people and the quantity
emitted per person. This graph shows the data of the per capita emissions of year 2020. The aim
of the visualization is to depict the correlation between population and the amount of CO2
emissions per capita. The countries with large populations, in this case above 5 million where
chosen for this graph.

The CO2 emissions per capita are shown y-axis, while x-axis depicts population. The radius of
the bubbles displays the GDP per capita of each country. The tooltip function provides information
about each country in detail, in order to help better understand the data.
